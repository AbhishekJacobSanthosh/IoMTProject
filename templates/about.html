IoMT-Blockchain Health Monitoring System

Welcome to the Future of Healthcare Data Management

This application demonstrates a novel approach to healthcare data management by integrating Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) devices with blockchain technology to create a secure, transparent, and patient-centric health monitoring system.

Key Features

  • Secure Health Data Storage - Immutable blockchain ledger prevents tampering
  • Real-time Health Monitoring - Continuous tracking of vital signs
  • Automated Health Alerts - Smart contracts detect abnormal readings
  • Patient Data Ownership - Patients control access to their health data
  • Healthcare Provider Integration - Secure sharing with authorized doctors

Research Foundation

Based on "A Survey of Distributed Ledger Technologies in the Health Domain"

This implementation is inspired by the research paper by Ikramullah Khan, Sudip Phuyal, Ricardo Correia, and Joao C. Ferreira (2024), which explores how distributed ledger technologies can address critical challenges in healthcare data management:

  • Data Fragmentation - Traditional systems operate as isolated silos
  • Security Concerns - Conventional EHRs are vulnerable to breaches
  • Limited Interoperability - Difficulty sharing data across systems
  • Patient Disempowerment - Patients have little control over their data

The paper surveys various blockchain applications in healthcare and identifies key frameworks that can transform health data management.

Paper Reference

Khan, I., Phuyal, S., Correia, R., & Ferreira, J. C. (2024). A Survey of Distributed Ledger Technologies in the Health Domain. Journal of Information and Automation Sciences, 19(6), 249-265. DOI: 10.2478/ias-2024-0017

What Makes Our Implementation Unique

Real-Time IoMT Integration

While the research paper discusses blockchain for EHRs, our implementation goes further by integrating Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) devices for real-time health monitoring. This creates a continuous, immutable health timeline that can be accessed by authorized providers or used for remote monitoring.

Smart contracts automatically analyze incoming data to detect abnormal readings, enabling proactive healthcare interventions.

Patient-Centric Design

Our system implements a strict role-based access control system that gives patients true ownership of their health data. Unlike traditional EHR systems where institutions control access, our blockchain implementation ensures:

  • Patients can view their complete medical history
  • Data cannot be altered retroactively
  • All access attempts are logged on the blockchain
  • Patients can grant and revoke access permissions

How to Use This System

For Patients
  1. Log in with your patient credentials
  2. View your health dashboard showing vital signs
  3. Monitor health trends over time through interactive charts
  4. Receive alerts when readings are outside normal ranges
  5. Access your complete medical history securely
For Healthcare Providers
  1. Log in with administrator credentials
  2. Register new patients in the system
  3. View and manage all patient records
  4. Generate health data for monitoring
  5. Explore the blockchain to verify data integrity
  6. Receive alerts for abnormal patient readings
